# The Risk of Delegating Cognition to LLMs

The emergence of Large Language Models has introduced a subtle but significant risk to our learning and thinking processes. When we delegate our cognitive processes to these AI systems, we may inadvertently sacrifice the deep understanding that comes from active engagement with information.

The primary danger lies not in using LLMs themselves, but in how we might be tempted to use them. These tools can generate coherent explanations, solve problems, and create structured content whether or not we genuinely engage with the material. This capability creates an illusion of learning without the necessary mental work that leads to true understanding.

Consider the difference between asking an LLM to explain a concept and working through that concept yourself with the LLM as a guide. In the first scenario, you might receive a perfect explanation but fail to integrate it into your existing knowledge. In the second scenario, you actively construct understanding through questioning, exploration, and connection-making.

The risk becomes particularly acute in academic and professional settings. When faced with complex problems, the temptation to let the LLM do the heavy cognitive lifting can be strong. However, this approach can lead to:

Understanding gaps that remain hidden until crucial moments
Difficulty applying knowledge in novel situations
Weakened ability to think critically and solve problems independently
Reduced capacity for creative and innovative thinking

The paradox is that while LLMs can make information more accessible and learning more efficient, they can also make it easier to avoid the meaningful cognitive work necessary for genuine learning. This is similar to how calculators, while invaluable tools, can hinder mathematical understanding if used to bypass rather than support the learning process.

To maintain healthy cognitive practices while working with LLMs, we must consciously choose to use them as tools for enhancing our thinking rather than replacing it. This means:

Engaging actively with the information they provide
Questioning and testing our understanding
Using them to explore different perspectives rather than accept single answers
Maintaining our own mental models and understanding frameworks

The goal is to harness the power of LLMs while preserving and strengthening our own cognitive capabilities. This requires a mindful approach to their use and a clear understanding of the difference between information access and genuine learning.
