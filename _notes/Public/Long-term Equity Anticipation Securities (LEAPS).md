### Understanding LEAPS

LEAPS are long-term options contracts, typically with expirations of one year or more. They provide extended time horizons for implementing various options strategies.

### Key Characteristics

1. Time Value Dynamics:
   - Slower [[Theta (Time Decay)|theta decay]] than shorter-term options
   - Greater sensitivity to interest rates
   - Significant vega exposure

2. Strategic Applications:
   - Leverage with reduced time decay pressure
   - Long-term directional exposure
   - Basis for complex option spreads

### Risk Considerations

3. Capital Efficiency:
   - Lower capital requirement than stock ownership
   - Higher leverage potential
   - Increased sensitivity to market movements

4. Greeks Exposure:
   - Substantial positive [[Vega (Volatility Sensitivity)|vega]]
   - Significant positive delta
   - Lower negative theta than short-term options

### Implementation Strategies

5. Directional Trading:
   - Alternative to stock ownership
   - Reduced capital requirement
   - Enhanced leverage potential

6. Spread Components:
   - PMCC basis
   - Calendar spreads
   - Diagonal spreads

7. Risk Management:
   - Position sizing considerations
   - Delta hedging approaches
   - Roll timing optimization

### Best Practices

8. Entry Considerations:
   - Evaluate implied volatility levels
   - Assess time value premium
   - Consider interest rate environment

9. Ongoing Management:
   - Monitor volatility changes
   - Track delta exposure
   - Evaluate roll opportunities

10. Exit Planning:
   - Determine profit targets
   - Set stop-loss levels
   - Plan roll strategies
